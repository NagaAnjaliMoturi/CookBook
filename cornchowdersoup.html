Corn Chowder Soup


Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives or green onions, chopped, for garnish

Process

  1. In a large pot, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until it's crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pot.
  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot with the bacon fat. Saut√© until the onion becomes translucent.
  3. Add the diced potatoes and corn kernels to the pot. Stir and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and add the dried thyme. Season with salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the potatoes are tender (about 15-20 minutes).
  5. Using an immersion blender, blend part of the soup until it's creamy but still has some chunks for texture. Alternatively, you can transfer a portion of the soup to a blender and then return it to the pot.
  6. Pour in the heavy cream and stir well. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. Serve the corn chowder hot, garnished with crispy bacon bits and chopped chives or green onions.
